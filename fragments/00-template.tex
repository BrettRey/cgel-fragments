\fragmenthead

\noindent
A fragment in this series describes a CGEL phenomenon as a class of trees
satisfying a formal specification, in the model-theoretic idiom of Rogers
(1998), the GPSG tradition (Gazdar, Klein, Pullum \& Sag 1985), and Pullum's
Edinburgh handouts. Each fragment is self-contained, two to four pages, and
proceeds in roughly the same shape:

\begin{enumerate}[label=(\arabic*),leftmargin=2em,itemsep=0.2em]
  \item \textbf{Phenomenon.} A CGEL construction or constraint, with a
        worked English example and the CGEL-style tree.
  \item \textbf{Vocabulary.} The unary and binary predicates needed
        beyond the shared ones in \texttt{shared/macros.tex}.
  \item \textbf{Specification.} The defining formula(e) in FOL or wMSO
        on trees, with glosses.
  \item \textbf{Worked instance.} The example from (1) shown to satisfy
        the specification.
  \item \textbf{Open questions.} Where the formalism strains, what
        richer logic might be needed, what coordination-style residues
        (à la Gazdar 1981) remain.
\end{enumerate}

\gloss{a vacancy is a phrasal node with no children}
\z
