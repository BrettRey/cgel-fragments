CGEL Fragments

A growing set of formal fragments describing constructions of The Cambridge Grammar of the English Language (Huddleston & Pullum 2002) in the model-theoretic idiom of logic on trees.

Each fragment is a short, self-contained handout (2–4 pages) that specifies a CGEL phenomenon as a class of trees satisfying a formula in first-order or weak monadic second-order logic, in the style of Pullum's Edinburgh "Advanced Topics in Syntax" handouts and the GPSG / model-theoretic syntax tradition (Rogers 1998, Gazdar et al. 1985).
